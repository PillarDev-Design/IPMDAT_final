Step 4 - Assessing the Feasibility of an Eradication
4.2 - Detection
Is the invasive plant always inconspicuous within the matrix vegetation (non-emergent with non-distinct features)?
Survey efforts will be problematic and extremely resource intensive if the invasive plant is difficult to detect. The detection effort is comprised of the resources required to delimit the infested area, find all the individuals for control, and survey for new occurences. One of the highest resource costs is the size of the infested area that must be searched.
